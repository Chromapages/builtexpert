# User Personas

## Purpose
These personas define the main types of users the BuiltExpert marketing website needs to serve. They exist to keep the project aligned with real buyer motivations, hesitations, and desired outcomes.

---

## Persona 1: The Growth-Minded Contractor Owner

### Snapshot
**Name:** Mike  
**Role:** Owner / Operator  
**Business Type:** HVAC, roofing, plumbing, electrical, or general home service business  
**Company Size:** 3–20 employees

### Goals
- get more qualified leads
- stop relying too heavily on referrals only
- look more credible online
- make it easier for homeowners to contact or book
- find a marketing partner that understands contractors

### Pain Points
- current website looks weak or outdated
- leads are inconsistent
- too many bad or low-intent leads
- past marketing providers sounded good but delivered vague results
- little time to manage a messy project

### What He Needs From The Site
- clear explanation of what BuiltExpert does
- confidence that the brand understands contractor lead generation
- proof that the process is organized
- simple path to start a conversation
- reassurance that support exists after launch

### Objections
- “Will this actually get me better leads?”
- “How much is this going to cost?”
- “Do they understand my trade?”
- “Am I going to be stuck doing a ton of work on my end?”

### Decision Drivers
- trust
- clarity
- relevance to contractors
- confidence in the process
- proof of business outcomes

## Persona 2: The Trade-Specific Growth Operator

### Snapshot
**Name:** Sarah  
**Role:** Co-owner / Operations Lead  
**Business Type:** Specialty trade company  
**Company Size:** 5–30 employees

### Goals
- improve lead quality
- support ads or local SEO with stronger pages
- modernize the company’s online presence
- work with someone dependable
- get a clearer growth system in place

### Pain Points
- current marketing is fragmented
- website is not helping conversion
- internal team is too busy to manage vendors closely
- messaging feels generic compared to stronger competitors
- local search visibility is weak

### What She Needs From The Site
- a clear explanation of services
- confidence that BuiltExpert can support lead generation, not just design
- process clarity
- proof and case studies
- a low-friction way to reach out

### Objections
- “Will this be another generic agency?”
- “Can they actually help with contractor-specific growth?”
- “Are they going to communicate clearly?”
- “Will this create more work for me?”

### Decision Drivers
- operational reliability
- practical strategy
- contractor relevance
- clarity of deliverables
- support after launch

---

## Persona 3: The Contractor Exploring An Audit First

### Snapshot
**Name:** Jose  
**Role:** Owner  
**Business Type:** Local contractor business  
**Company Size:** 1–10 employees

### Goals
- understand what is broken in current lead flow
- get a practical outside opinion before committing to a bigger project
- reduce wasted spend
- find a trustworthy next step

### Pain Points
- not sure whether the real problem is website, ads, SEO, or messaging
- skeptical of agencies
- has been burned before
- not ready to commit to a big engagement without clarity

### What He Needs From The Site
- a clear audit offer
- a sense that the audit will be practical and useful
- simple explanation of what happens next
- proof that BuiltExpert understands contractor growth

### Objections
- “Is this audit just a sales pitch?”
- “Will I actually get useful insight?”
- “Is this worth paying attention to?”
- “Will they understand my business?”

### Decision Drivers
- low-risk entry point
- practical value
- straightforward language
- relevance to contractor pain points

---

## Shared Patterns Across Personas

### What All Personas Want
- a credible partner
- more qualified leads
- simple next steps
- confidence in process
- reassurance that the project will not become chaotic

### What All Personas Fear
- wasting money
- hiring the wrong provider
- getting vague marketing advice
- ending up with a better-looking site that still does not perform
- being left unsupported after launch

### What The Website Must Communicate To All Of Them
- BuiltExpert understands contractor growth
- the process is clear
- the offer is practical
- the work is trustworthy
- the next step is easy and low-friction
