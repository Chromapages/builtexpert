# BuiltExpert Pricing Architecture

## Purpose
This document defines the internal pricing architecture for BuiltExpert.

This is not the polished public pricing page.  
This is the internal pricing logic that determines:

- what each offer costs directionally
- which offers can be sold with fixed or semi-fixed pricing
- which offers should be custom
- what each package includes
- what each package excludes
- when an audit should happen before quoting
- how offers should upsell into each other

Without this layer, the sales process becomes vague, slow, and inconsistent.

---

## 1. Pricing Philosophy

BuiltExpert should not compete on being cheap.

It should compete on:
- contractor relevance
- clarity
- better lead quality
- stronger conversion
- practical growth outcomes
- easier implementation than a generic agency

### Core Pricing Rule
The price should reflect that BuiltExpert is helping contractors get more qualified leads, not just giving them “a website.”

### Strategic Position
BuiltExpert should sit in the market as:
- more specialized than a generic freelancer
- more practical than a bloated agency
- more outcome-focused than a design-only shop
- premium enough to attract serious buyers
- simple enough to still feel accessible

---

## 2. Official Offer Stack

BuiltExpert pricing should follow this order:

1. **Lead System Audit**
2. **Contractor Websites**
3. **Landing Pages**
4. **Local SEO**
5. **Growth Support**

Each offer has a different pricing logic. That is important.

---

## 3. Pricing Model By Offer

## Offer 1 — Lead System Audit

### Pricing Model
**Fixed or semi-fixed**

### Why
This is an entry offer. It should be easy to understand and easy to buy.

### Recommended Range
- **$297–$750**

### Suggested Positioning
Use a single clean anchor instead of overcomplicating it.

### My Recommendation
Start with:
- **$497** as the main audit offer

That is high enough to filter out unserious leads, but still accessible enough to act as an entry point.

### What’s Included
- website review
- messaging review
- CTA/conversion review
- local SEO baseline review
- trust/credibility review
- prioritized findings
- practical next-step recommendations

### What’s Excluded
- implementation
- full strategy workshops
- ad management
- repeated consulting rounds

### Pricing Notes
If you later want tiers, keep them light:
- Basic Audit
- Deep Audit

But I would start with one clean price first.

## Offer 2 — Contractor Websites

### Pricing Model
**Tiered with custom upper-end scope**

### Why
This is the flagship offer and will vary by:
- number of pages
- service structure
- content complexity
- integrations
- trade-specific needs
- timeline
- whether copywriting is included

### Recommended Structure
Use three pricing levels:

#### Tier 1 — Starter Website
Best for:
- smaller contractors
- owner-operators
- businesses needing a clean, credible web presence fast

Suggested range:
- **$2,500–$4,500**

Typical inclusions:
- smaller page count
- core service pages
- contact flow
- basic SEO structure
- mobile-first implementation
- launch QA

#### Tier 2 — Growth Website
Best for:
- contractors actively trying to improve lead generation
- businesses with multiple services
- companies needing stronger messaging and conversion structure

Suggested range:
- **$5,000–$8,500**

Typical inclusions:
- expanded page structure
- stronger service architecture
- conversion-focused messaging
- deeper trust/proof structure
- stronger local SEO foundation
- more strategic build scope

#### Tier 3 — Custom / Authority Website
Best for:
- more established contractors
- multi-location businesses
- companies needing larger page sets or more complexity
- more aggressive growth positioning

Suggested range:
- **$9,000+**

Typical inclusions:
- broader sitemap
- more complex content structure
- advanced service area strategy
- deeper integration or more custom needs
- more involved project planning

### What’s Included In Website Pricing
At minimum:
- structure
- core build
- mobile responsiveness
- lead-focused CTA setup
- contact flow
- launch QA
- baseline SEO implementation

### What’s Usually Not Included
Unless explicitly added:
- ongoing SEO
- ad management
- unlimited revisions
- custom CRM work
- extensive copywriting if not scoped
- advanced integrations
- photography/video production

### Critical Rule
Do not let every website project default to custom pricing immediately.  
Use tiers first, then custom scope when truly necessary.

#### Landing Page Sprint / Multi-Page Set
Suggested range:
- **$2,000–$4,000+**

Best for:
- multiple service pages
- campaign clusters
- ad-specific page sets
- seasonal pushes

### What’s Included
- focused page structure
- message-to-offer alignment
- CTA setup
- form/booking integration
- tracking setup
- launch QA

### What’s Excluded

## Offer 4 — Local SEO

### Pricing Model
**Monthly recurring, with optional setup fee**

### Why
Local SEO is not a one-time deliverable. It needs ongoing work, monitoring, and improvement.

### Recommended Structure

#### Tier 1 — Local SEO Starter
Suggested monthly range:
- **$500–$900/mo**

Best for:
- smaller contractors
- lower competition markets
- baseline local visibility improvement

Typical inclusions:
- on-page local improvements
- service/page recommendations
- core local SEO fixes
- basic reporting

#### Tier 2 — Growth Local SEO
Suggested monthly range:
- **$1,000–$1,750/mo**

Best for:
- stronger growth intent
- more competitive local markets
- businesses needing more active local SEO work

Typical inclusions:
- deeper on-page work
- stronger service area content guidance
- optimization support
- stronger local visibility strategy
- recurring reporting and recommendations

#### Tier 3 — Competitive Market SEO
Suggested monthly range:
- **$2,000+/mo**

Best for:
- competitive cities
- multi-location contractors
- more aggressive growth environments

### Optional Setup Fee
Recommended:
- **$500–$1,500** depending on audit findings and starting condition

### What’s Included
- local SEO baseline and improvements
- service-area relevance support
- on-page/local optimization
- performance and visibility review
- recurring recommendations

### What’s Excluded
- instant rankings
- massive content production unless scoped
- national SEO
- paid ads

### Critical Rule
Do not underprice Local SEO just because it is monthly.  
Recurring work still needs margin.

Best for:
- lighter website updates
- smaller improvement cycles
- basic support needs

#### Tier 2 — Growth
Suggested monthly range:
- **$1,250–$2,000/mo**

Best for:
- active iteration
- landing page additions
- conversion improvements
- ongoing website and content work

#### Tier 3 — Expansion
Suggested monthly range:
- **$2,500+/mo**

Best for:
- larger support needs
- more frequent deliverables
- ongoing growth execution across multiple fronts

### What’s Included
Possible inclusions:
- website updates
- page additions
- landing page work
- CTA/conversion improvements
- content revisions
- SEO improvements if scoped

### What’s Excluded

- emergency all-hours support

## 4. Audit Requirement Rules

Not every lead should jump straight into a proposal.

## Require Audit First When:
- the lead does not know what the real problem is
- scope is unclear
- website + SEO + messaging all appear broken
- the client wants deep advice before committing
- there are too many unknowns to quote responsibly

## Allow Direct Proposal When:
- the problem is obvious
- scope is narrow
- the lead is referred and highly qualified
- the right offer is already clear
- there is enough information to quote responsibly

### Recommendation
Use the audit as the default diagnostic gate when clarity is low.  
Do not use it as unnecessary friction when the right next step is already obvious.

---

## 5. Internal Pricing Rules

### Rule 1
Do not quote from emotion. Quote from the offer structure.

### Rule 2
Use pricing ranges to anchor, then refine with scope.

### Rule 3
Only go fully custom when the work genuinely exceeds tier logic.

### Rule 4
Do not collapse price just to close weak-fit leads.

### Rule 5
If a buyer cannot afford the right offer, do not distort the scope until it becomes weak and unprofitable.

## 7. What Should Be Public vs Internal

## Public Pricing Guidance
The website can safely show:
- starting prices
- pricing ranges
- “starting at” language
- tier names
- what affects pricing
- who each offer is for

## Internal Pricing Guidance
Keep these more detailed:
- custom quote thresholds
- margin expectations
- discount rules
- exact scoping logic
- add-on pricing
- deal-floor thresholds

### Recommendation
Public pricing should qualify.  
Internal pricing should protect margin.

---

## 8. Discount Policy Guidance

BuiltExpert should avoid becoming discount-driven.

### Acceptable Discount Situations
- referral relationships
- strategic case-study opportunities
- bundled work with higher lifetime value
- fast yes / low-friction deals if margin remains strong

### Bad Discount Situations
- prospect pushes back without real reason
- weak-fit lead wants cheaper work
- scope is already too thin
- price is being used to compensate for unclear value

### Rule
Discount only when the business case is strong.  
Do not discount because the sales message is weak.
