# BuiltExpert Sales Architecture

## Purpose
This document defines how BuiltExpert should turn traffic into qualified conversations and paying clients.

The offer architecture defines **what** BuiltExpert sells.  
This document defines **how people buy it**.

That means:
- which CTA should show up first
- which offer should be recommended in which situation
- how leads should be qualified
- how the website should route people
- how sales conversations should stay clear instead of turning into vague custom consulting

---

## 1. Core Sales Principle

BuiltExpert should not sell like a broad agency.

It should sell like a contractor growth system with a clear progression:

1. **Attract**
2. **Qualify**
3. **Diagnose**
4. **Recommend**
5. **Close**
6. **Expand**

If the website and sales process do not support that sequence, the brand will feel vague no matter how good the service list is.

---

## 2. The Main Sales Paths

BuiltExpert should have two primary entry paths.

## Path A — Direct Discovery Call
This is for visitors who already have enough intent and trust to talk now.

### Best For
- referred leads
- warmer traffic
- contractors who already know they need help
- prospects ready to discuss scope and budget

### Primary CTA
**Book A Call**

### Goal
Move the lead into a qualified sales conversation quickly.

---

## Path B — Lead System Audit
This is the lower-friction path for skeptical or less certain buyers.

### Best For
- colder traffic
- contractors who are unsure what is broken
- prospects burned by agencies before
- leads who need a practical first step before committing

### Primary CTA
**Start The Audit**

### Goal
Use the audit to diagnose the real issue and convert the prospect into a larger engagement.

---

## 3. Official CTA Hierarchy

This needs to stay consistent across the site.

## Primary CTA
**Book A Call**

This should remain the main CTA on the homepage and most core pages.

## Secondary CTA
**Start The Audit** or **View The Audit**

This should be the main lower-friction path.

## Why This Is Right
If audit becomes the only dominant CTA, BuiltExpert may accidentally attract too many low-commitment people.  
If Book A Call is the only path, colder prospects may bounce because they are not ready yet.

So the system should be:

- **Book A Call** for high-intent prospects
- **Start The Audit** for diagnostic entry

That is the correct dual-path structure.

---

## 4. Offer Routing Logic

The sales system needs rules for which offer to recommend.

## Route To Lead System Audit When:
- the lead is unsure what the problem is
- the website, SEO, messaging, and lead flow all may be involved
- the buyer is skeptical and needs proof first
- the lead is not ready to commit to a full project
- you need clearer diagnosis before scoping

## Route To Contractor Websites When:
- the site is obviously outdated
- messaging is weak
- service presentation is poor
- conversion structure is broken
- the lead already knows the website is the main issue

## Route To Landing Pages When:
- the prospect needs campaign pages
- the business wants to promote one service or one offer
- paid traffic is involved
- a full site rebuild is not the first move
- there is a fast-turnaround opportunity

## Route To Local SEO When:
- the lead needs stronger local visibility
- map/local search presence is weak
- service-area visibility is poor
- local intent traffic is being missed

## Route To Growth Support When:
- the client already completed a project
- the prospect wants ongoing iteration and improvements
- multiple services need to be maintained together
- long-term support makes more sense than one-off work

---

## 5. Homepage Sales Role

The homepage should not try to explain every nuance of every offer equally.

Its sales job is to do four things:

1. Make BuiltExpert instantly legible
2. Establish contractor-specific trust
3. Show the main offer categories
4. Move the visitor into the right entry path

### Homepage Conversion Priority
1. **Book A Call**
2. **Start The Audit**
3. **View Work**
4. Everything else supports those actions

### Critical Rule
The homepage is not the place to over-explain.  
It is the place to make the next step feel obvious.

---

## 6. Sales Funnel Structure

## Funnel 1 — Direct Sales Funnel
Homepage / Service Page / Work / Pricing  
→ Book A Call  
→ Qualification Conversation  
→ Recommended Offer  
→ Close  
→ Growth Support later

## Funnel 2 — Audit-Led Funnel
Homepage / Audit Page / Service Page  
→ Start The Audit  
→ Audit Delivery  
→ Recommendation  
→ Close Into Website / Landing Pages / Local SEO  
→ Growth Support later

### Why Two Funnels Matter
This creates flexibility without confusion.

Some buyers want to talk immediately.  
Some buyers need a lower-risk first step.

BuiltExpert should support both.

---

## 7. Qualification Framework

The website and sales process should qualify leads against five things:

## 1. Fit
Are they the kind of contractor or trade business BuiltExpert wants?

## 2. Problem Clarity
Do they know the problem, or do they need diagnosis first?

## 3. Readiness
Are they ready to move, or are they just browsing?

## 4. Budget Reality
Do they have enough budget to be serious?

## 5. Implementation Need
Do they actually need execution, or just advice?

---

## 8. Website Qualification Questions

These should show up in forms or be answered during the first call.

### Recommended Qualification Inputs
- what kind of business do you run?
- what do you need help with?
- what is the biggest lead-generation problem right now?
- what is your rough budget range?
- what is your timeline?
- do you already have a website?
- are you currently doing SEO or paid traffic?

### Why These Matter
These questions help determine:
- whether the lead should go to audit or direct project
- whether the project is website, landing page, or SEO-led
- how serious the lead is
- how to frame the next step

---

## 9. Sales Call Architecture

A sales call should not be an open-ended brainstorm session.

It should follow a structure.

## Call Sequence
1. Understand the business
2. Understand the current lead problem
3. Diagnose the likely bottleneck
4. Recommend the right offer
5. Frame expected outcome
6. Discuss fit, scope, and budget
7. Close next step

### What To Avoid
- generic agency talk
- offering every possible service
- turning the call into free consulting
- scoping before diagnosis is clear
- saying yes to bad-fit work

- help contractor place themselves in the right service category

## Audit

- convert uncertain or skeptical leads into a real entry offer

## Work

## Process

## Pricing
Primary role:
- help visitors self-qualify

## Contact
Primary role:
- convert warm intent with low friction

---

## 11. Pricing In The Sales Process

Pricing should not float around vaguely.

It should be used to help qualify and frame the next step.

## Recommended Pricing Logic By Offer

### Lead System Audit
Use as:
- fixed or semi-fixed entry price

### Contractor Websites
Use as:
- premium project offer with defined tiers or starting ranges

### Landing Pages
Use as:
- smaller focused project or sprint

### Local SEO
Use as:
- monthly engagement or staged SEO offer

### Growth Support
Use as:
- recurring retainer with clear scope logic

### Critical Rule
Do not let every deal become “custom pricing, depends on everything.”  
That weakens trust and slows sales.

---

## 12. Sales Expansion Logic

The real long-term value in BuiltExpert is not just initial project revenue. It is expansion.

## Typical Expansion Path
Lead System Audit  
→ Contractor Website  
→ Landing Pages  
→ Local SEO  
→ Growth Support

Or:

Contractor Website  
→ Landing Pages  
→ Growth Support

Or:

Local SEO  
→ Website improvements  
→ Growth Support

### Why This Matters
The sales system should not think in isolated projects only.  
It should think in lead-to-lifetime-value paths.

---

## 13. What The Site Should Not Do

The site should not:
- try to sell every offer equally hard on every page
- present a giant agency menu
- overwhelm visitors with too many CTAs
- bury the audit path
- bury the direct call path
- sound like generic digital marketing fluff

---

## 14. Recommended Official Sales Structure

## Main Public Position
BuiltExpert helps contractors get more qualified leads through clearer websites, focused landing pages, local SEO, and practical growth support.

## Main CTA
**Book A Call**

## Main Lower-Friction Entry
**Start The Audit**

## Website Sales Spine
- clarify the problem
- show contractor relevance
- build trust
- route to the right next step
- qualify before scoping
- close into the correct offer
- expand into Growth Support later

---

## 15. Strategic Recommendation

The next smartest move after this document is to define the **pricing architecture** in the same structured way.

That means:
- package names
- starting prices or ranges
- what each tier includes
- what each tier excludes
- which services are fixed vs custom
- which offers require an audit first

Once pricing logic is clear, the homepage hierarchy and sales pages will get much sharper.
