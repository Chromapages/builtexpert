# BuiltExpert Offer Architecture

## Purpose
This document defines the official offer architecture for BuiltExpert. Its job is to turn the service list into a real buying system that is easier to sell, easier to understand, and easier to deliver.

This is not just a service menu. It is the structure behind how BuiltExpert should acquire, qualify, convert, and expand contractor clients.

---

## 1. Core Offer Strategy

BuiltExpert should be positioned around a simple progression:

1. **Diagnose**
2. **Fix**
3. **Grow**

That means the business should not present itself like a broad, generic agency. It should present itself like a contractor growth partner with a clear path.

### Official Offer Ladder

## Entry Offer
- **Lead System Audit**

## Core Delivery Offers
- **Contractor Websites**
- **Landing Pages**
- **Local SEO**

## Continuity Offer
- **Growth Support**

This is the cleanest version of the BuiltExpert offer stack.

---

## 2. Why This Structure Is Right

This offer architecture works because it matches how contractor buyers actually think.

### Buyer Type 1
“I know my website is weak.”
- Buys: **Contractor Websites**

### Buyer Type 2
“I need more leads from ads, offers, or service pages.”
- Buys: **Landing Pages**

### Buyer Type 3
“I need to show up more locally.”
- Buys: **Local SEO**

### Buyer Type 4
“I don’t know what’s broken yet.”
- Buys: **Lead System Audit**

### Buyer Type 5
“I want ongoing help improving things.”
- Buys: **Growth Support**

That buying logic is simple, intuitive, and easy to map across the website.

---

## 3. Offer 1 — Lead System Audit

## Role In The Offer Ladder
This is the official entry offer.

## Purpose
Help a contractor understand what is actually holding back lead generation before they commit to a larger project.

## Who It’s For
Best fit for:
- contractors who know growth is stuck but are not sure why
- businesses with an outdated website or unclear lead flow
- owners burned by generic agencies in the past
- contractors who want a lower-risk first step

Not ideal for:
- people wanting free consulting disguised as an audit
- prospects with no real business or growth intent
- buyers who only want the cheapest possible option

## Problem It Solves
Most contractors do not know whether the real issue is:
- their website
- their messaging
- their local visibility
- their landing pages
- their conversion flow
- or their broader lead system

The audit creates clarity.

## What’s Included
Recommended inclusions:
- website review
- messaging review
- CTA/conversion review
- local SEO baseline review
- trust/credibility review
- lead flow friction review
- prioritized findings
- practical next-step recommendations

## Deliverable
The audit should result in:
- a short written report, Loom, PDF, or hybrid deliverable
- prioritized issues
- practical next actions
- recommendation on whether BuiltExpert should implement the fixes

## What It Is Not
It is not:
- a full website redesign
- free strategy consulting
- ongoing SEO work
- ad management
- endless back-and-forth consultation

## Timeline
Recommended turnaround:
- 3 to 5 business days
- or up to 7 business days if needed operationally

## Pricing Logic
Recommended pricing direction:
- low enough to be a credible entry point
- high enough to filter out non-serious people

Suggested range:
- **$297–$750** depending on depth and niche focus

### My Recommendation
Start with a clean mid-range offer, not too cheap.
If it is too cheap, the buyer will not respect it and you will attract the wrong prospects.

## CTA
- **Start The Audit**
- Secondary: **Book A Call**

## Upsell Path
The audit should naturally lead into:
- Contractor Websites
- Landing Pages
- Local SEO
- Growth Support

## Strategic Role
This is the trust-builder and diagnostic wedge. It should make sales easier, not harder.

## 4. Offer 2 — Contractor Websites

## Role In The Offer Ladder
This is the flagship core delivery offer.

## Purpose
Build contractor-focused websites that improve credibility, clarify the offer, and convert more visitors into qualified leads.

## Who It’s For
Best fit for:
- contractors with outdated or weak websites
- businesses getting traffic but poor conversion
- contractors investing in SEO, referrals, or ads that need a better destination
- owners who want a more serious online presence

Not ideal for:
- people who only want a cheap template
- businesses without a real offer or service structure
- prospects who do not value content, strategy, or approvals

## Problem It Solves
A lot of contractor websites:
- look outdated
- feel generic
- do not build trust
- do not explain services clearly
- do not convert mobile traffic well
- waste traffic and ad spend

This offer solves that.

## What’s Included
Recommended inclusions:
- messaging and page structure
- high-conversion website build
- clear service presentation
- lead-focused CTA structure
- mobile-first implementation
- basic SEO foundations
- contact and conversion flow setup
- launch QA

## Deliverable
A completed contractor-focused website built to:
- look credible
- load fast
- communicate clearly
- generate more qualified inquiries

## What It Is Not
It is not:
- a giant custom app build
- full ad management
- an all-in-one marketing package
- every possible add-on bundled together

## Timeline
Recommended range:
- **3 to 6 weeks** depending on scope

## Pricing Logic
This should be a premium but still practical offer.

Suggested structure:
- entry website package
- growth website package
- custom/high-scope package

### Range Direction
Do not position this as bargain-bin.  
The site should be framed as a lead-generation asset, not a cosmetic expense.

## CTA
- **Book A Call**
- Secondary: **Start With An Audit**

## Upsell Path
Natural upsells:
- Landing Pages
- Local SEO
- Growth Support

## Strategic Role
This is the main revenue anchor of BuiltExpert.

---

## 5. Offer 3 — Landing Pages

## Role In The Offer Ladder
This is the campaign and quick-win offer.

## Purpose
Create focused pages for offers, service campaigns, paid traffic, seasonal pushes, and service-area lead generation.

## Who It’s For
Best fit for:
- contractors running or planning ads
- businesses promoting one service hard
- seasonal promotions
- service-area or niche-specific campaigns
- owners who need results faster than a full website rebuild

Not ideal for:
- contractors with no clear offer
- businesses expecting a landing page alone to fix everything
- buyers who actually need a full site first

## Problem It Solves
Contractors often send traffic to pages that:
- are too broad
- lack clear CTAs
- do not match the campaign
- do not convert well

Landing pages solve that mismatch.

## What’s Included
Recommended inclusions:
- focused page structure
- message-to-offer alignment
- conversion-focused CTA setup
- proof/trust placement
- form or booking integration
- tracking setup
- launch QA

## Deliverable
A focused page or small page set designed to convert a specific traffic source or offer.

## What It Is Not
It is not:
- a substitute for a broken full website in every case
- a full ad management service
- a complete SEO program

## Timeline
Recommended range:
- **5 to 10 business days** for simpler scopes
- longer if part of a broader system

## Pricing Logic
Should be priced as:
- fast
- focused
- high-value
- lower scope than a full website

This can also be one of the easiest offers to sell after an audit.

## CTA
- **Book A Call**
- Secondary: **Start With An Audit**

## Upsell Path
Natural upsells:
- Contractor Websites
- Growth Support
- Local SEO if local organic intent matters

## Strategic Role
This is the practical campaign lever in the stack.

---

## 6. Offer 4 — Local SEO

## Role In The Offer Ladder
This is the high-intent visibility offer.

## Purpose
Help contractors improve local search visibility and capture more high-intent traffic from buyers already searching for services.

## Who It’s For
Best fit for:
- contractors who rely on local market visibility
- businesses needing stronger map and local organic presence
- contractors with weak service-area search presence
- owners who want a more sustainable inbound channel

Not ideal for:
- businesses unwilling to commit long enough for SEO work to matter
- contractors with weak website foundations that need fixing first
- buyers expecting instant results from local SEO alone

## Problem It Solves
A contractor can have a decent service and still lose business if they:
- do not show up locally
- have weak local relevance signals
- have poor service-area pages
- fail to convert search traffic when it arrives

## What’s Included
Recommended inclusions:
- local SEO baseline setup
- on-page local optimization
- service/location page recommendations
- Google Business Profile alignment where relevant
- internal linking and content recommendations
- reporting or visibility tracking
- ongoing improvement path

## Deliverable
A local SEO engagement aimed at increasing visibility and improving the quality of inbound local traffic.

## What It Is Not
It is not:
- overnight rankings
- broad national SEO
- content farm blogging
- magic-button lead generation

## Timeline
SEO should be framed as an ongoing offer, not a one-week fix.

## Pricing Logic
This should likely be:
- monthly
- scoped by competitiveness, geography, and execution depth

It can be sold:
- standalone
- after a website build
- after an audit

## CTA
- **Book A Call**
- Secondary: **Start With An Audit**

## Upsell Path
Natural upsells:
- Contractor Websites
- Landing Pages
- Growth Support

## Strategic Role
This is one of the strongest recurring-revenue offers in the stack.

---

## 7. Offer 5 — Growth Support

## Role In The Offer Ladder
This is the continuity and expansion offer.

## Purpose
Keep a client’s site, pages, SEO, and lead-generation system improving after the initial project is complete.

## Who It’s For
Best fit for:
- clients who want ongoing help
- businesses running campaigns and needing iteration
- contractors who do not want their site to stagnate
- clients needing updates, additions, and monthly improvement work

Not ideal for:
- buyers wanting one-off emergency work only
- prospects who do not value iteration
- businesses that are not ready for a recurring engagement

## Problem It Solves
Most websites and growth systems stall after launch because nobody keeps improving them.

## What’s Included
Potential inclusions:
- website updates
- new landing pages
- CTA and conversion improvements
- SEO improvements
- content updates
- monthly performance and next-step recommendations

## Deliverable
A recurring relationship focused on keeping the lead-generation system active and improving.

## What It Is Not
It is not:
- unlimited everything
- a catch-all agency retainer
- a substitute for clear scope

## Timeline
Monthly recurring engagement.

## Pricing Logic
Should be tiered based on:
- scope
- response speed
- volume of changes
- whether SEO work is included
- whether landing page builds are included

## CTA
- **Talk About Ongoing Support**
- Secondary: **Book A Call**

## Upsell Path
This is usually the end-state offer after:
- websites
- landing pages
- local SEO
- audit-driven implementation work

## Strategic Role
This is the lifetime-value engine.

Start with:
- Lead System Audit

### If the prospect clearly needs a better site
Start with:
- Contractor Websites

### If the prospect needs focused campaign pages
Start with:
- Landing Pages

### If the prospect wants stronger local visibility
Start with:
- Local SEO

### If the prospect is already a client or wants ongoing help
Start with:
- Growth Support

---

## 10. Pricing Logic Guidance

This is internal positioning guidance, not final public pricing.

## Lead System Audit
- lower-ticket entry offer
- should still filter out low-quality prospects

## Contractor Websites
- main premium project offer
- strongest project-based revenue line

## Landing Pages
- smaller, focused project offer
- practical quick-win option

## Local SEO
- recurring or staged engagement
- supports long-term visibility and lead flow

## Growth Support
- recurring support/iteration retainer
- expands lifetime value

### Critical Rule
Do not let pricing become so custom and vague that the buyer cannot place themselves in the offer stack.  
And do not make it so rigid that every project becomes a mismatch.

---

## 11. What BuiltExpert Should Not Sell Yet

To keep the positioning sharp, BuiltExpert should avoid adding these too early:

- social media management
- general branding packages
- broad graphic design services
- full ad management as a front-and-center service
- generic “digital marketing” language
- automation / CRM setup as a core public offer
- broad agency menus

These may become support capabilities later, but they should not dilute the brand now.

---

## 12. Strategic Recommendation

BuiltExpert should be sold as a **contractor lead generation and growth brand**, not a generalist agency.

That means the business should feel like:
- specialized
- practical
- growth-focused
- easy to understand
- structured around real contractor buying behavior

The cleanest built-out v1 is:

- **Lead System Audit**
- **Contractor Websites**
- **Landing Pages**
- **Local SEO**
- **Growth Support**

That is enough to sell clearly, deliver well, and expand later without bloating the brand.
