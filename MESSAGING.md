# BuiltExpert — Messaging Stack

> **One-line brand position:**
> BuiltExpert helps contractors generate more qualified leads and turn more of those leads into booked jobs.

---

## 1. Core Positioning

BuiltExpert is a **contractor growth brand**, not a web design company.

The distinction matters in every piece of copy:

| Sound like this | Not this |
|---|---|
| "A lead generation and conversion system for contractors" | "A web design agency" |
| "We get your phone ringing with better jobs" | "We build beautiful websites" |
| "More booked work, less chasing bad leads" | "We offer digital marketing services" |

The positioning works because:
- Contractors are outcome-driven. They want calls and jobs, not deliverables.
- "System" signals reliability and repeatability — what every contractor craves after years of inconsistent pipelines.
- It separates us from generic agencies who could serve anyone.

---

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary persona

**The owner-operator running a small to midsize contracting business.**

- Handles sales, operations, and field work simultaneously
- Growth is happening but it feels unpredictable
- Too dependent on word-of-mouth and referrals
- Has tried marketing before and felt burned
- Doesn't want more tools — wants outcomes he can measure in the field

### Best-fit verticals (lead with these)

1. HVAC
2. Roofing
3. Plumbing
4. Remodeling
5. General Contractors

These verticals share the same pain profile: high job value, geographically bound, high competition for local search, and a strong word-of-mouth culture that caps growth.

## 3. Customer Pain Points

Hit these in headlines, sub-copy, problem sections, and CTAs. These are the recognition triggers that make a contractor stop scrolling.

| Pain | How to phrase it |
|---|---|
| Not enough qualified leads | "Your leads are inconsistent — some weeks slammed, some weeks silent" |
| Bad leads wasting time | "Too many price shoppers and tire-kickers eating your calendar" |
| Over-reliance on referrals | "Still waiting on the next referral to come in?" |
| Outdated website | "Your website looks fine but it's not doing anything for you" |
| Poor Google visibility | "Your competitors are ranking above you and getting your calls" |
| Missed calls and slow follow-up | "You're losing jobs because you can't get back to leads fast enough" |
| Inconsistent pipeline | "Feast or famine — busy one month, scrambling the next" |
| Unclear marketing ROI | "You've spent money on marketing and have no idea what it did" |

**Rule:** Always pair the pain with what it costs them. Not "you have a bad website" — "you have a bad website and it's costing you jobs every month."

## 5. Messaging Pillars

Use these four pillars to structure every major content asset — pages, ads, emails, sales conversations.

---

### Pillar 1 — More Qualified Leads

**The point:** Not volume. Quality.

Most contractors have been burned by lead gen platforms (HomeAdvisor, Angi, Thumbtack) that sell the same lead to five competitors. We build a proprietary system that generates leads exclusive to them.

**Copy directions:**
- "Exclusive leads — we never sell your lead to a competitor"
- "Not more traffic. Better traffic."
- "More calls from people who already want what you do"
- "Stop paying for leads that go nowhere"

**Avoid:** Impressions, click-throughs, traffic volume. Contractors don't care. They care about the phone ringing.

---

### Pillar 2 — Built for Contractors

**The point:** This is not a generic agency.

Contractors are skeptical of agencies for good reason. Most have been burned by someone who promised results and delivered reports. The "built for contractors" pillar signals that we know their world — the seasonality, the local search dynamics, the CRM tools they use, the jobs they want to land.

**Copy directions:**
- "We know the difference between an HVAC split system and a panel upgrade"
- "Trade-specific — not copy-paste marketing"
- "We've done this for HVAC, electrical, roofing, plumbing, and remodeling"
- "We integrate directly with ServiceTitan and Housecall Pro"
- "Your competitors are our reference point, not some generic benchmark"

**Avoid:** Generic agency language like "data-driven solutions" or "360° digital strategy."

---

### Pillar 3 — Done-for-You Simplicity

**The point:** Owners do not want more work. They want outcomes handed to them.

The best contractor clients are busy. They don't want to log into dashboards, review reports, or manage vendors. They want to tell us their goal and come back to more leads. Our messaging should reflect this.

**Copy directions:**
- "You run the jobs. We bring the calls."
- "No dashboards to manage. No guesswork. Just results."
- "We handle the build, the SEO, the content, and the follow-up"
- "Set it up once. Watch the system work."
- "We send you a simple monthly report — calls, leads, jobs booked"

**Avoid:** Feature-first language. Don't lead with "we do SEO and CRO and PPC." Lead with the outcome, then explain the mechanism if needed.

---

### Pillar 4 — Better Follow-Up and Conversion

**The point:** Leads that don't convert into calls are worthless.

Most contractors lose 30–50% of potential jobs not because the lead was bad, but because follow-up was slow or non-existent. A contractor in the field can't always answer the phone. We build systems that follow up automatically so no lead goes cold.

**Copy directions:**
- "Getting leads isn't enough if they don't turn into booked jobs"
- "We build the system that follows up while you're on the job site"
- "Stop losing work to slow response times"
- "Every form fill, every missed call — captured and followed up"
- "Close more of the leads you're already getting"

**Avoid:** Framing this as an add-on or optional feature. Follow-up and conversion is central to the system, not a bonus.

## 6. Voice and Tone

BuiltExpert speaks like a trusted expert in the field — not a corporate agency, not a bro-marketer.

| Tone attribute | Means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct** | Say the thing. No fluff. | "Your website isn't generating leads. Here's why." |
| **Trade-knowledgeable** | Reference the contractor's world specifically | "Panel upgrades, emergency HVAC calls, reroof estimates" |
| **Results-first** | Lead with outcomes, then explain how | "19 calls last month" before "we optimised your GBP" |
| **Honest** | Don't oversell. Acknowledge the work required. | "Results take time. Most clients see real movement in 60–90 days." |
| **Confident, not arrogant** | We know what works. We don't brag. | "This is what we build. Here's what it does." |

### Words to use
calls · jobs · booked work · qualified leads · local search · service areas · estimate requests · pipeline · field · trade · system · exclusive · predictable · conversion

### Words to avoid
synergy · solutions · leverage · seamless · holistic · digital-first · data-driven (as a headline) · impressions · clicks · traffic (as end goals)
