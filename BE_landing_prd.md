# PRD — BuiltExpert HVAC Lead System Audit Landing Page

## Document Info
- **Product:** BuiltExpert
- **Page:** HVAC Lead System Audit Landing Page
- **Page Type:** Conversion-focused single-offer landing page
- **Primary Goal:** Sell the HVAC Lead System Audit
- **Secondary Goal:** Build trust and qualify leads for future Starter / Growth / OS packages
- **Audience:** HVAC business owners, operators, or decision-makers evaluating why their current website and lead flow are underperforming

---

# 1. Overview

The HVAC Lead System Audit landing page is a dedicated sales page designed to convert HVAC businesses into buyers of BuiltExpert’s entry offer: the **HVAC Lead System Audit**.

This page should communicate the value of the audit clearly, reduce friction, explain the deliverable, build trust, and drive direct purchase intent. The page is not meant to educate broadly about all BuiltExpert services. It is meant to sell one fixed-scope diagnostic offer.

The audit is the front-end product that validates demand, brings in initial revenue, surfaces client pain points, and creates a natural path into higher-tier BuiltExpert offers.

---

# 2. Objective

## Primary Objective
Convert visitors into audit buyers.

## Secondary Objectives
- Establish BuiltExpert as a contractor-growth specialist
- Position the audit as a low-risk, high-value first step
- Create a strong bridge into future implementation packages
- Build credibility with HVAC-specific messaging
- Pre-qualify prospects who are a fit for higher-value services later

# 3. Business Context

BuiltExpert is moving toward a larger vision as a contractor growth brand and future operating system. The HVAC Lead System Audit is the first practical offer that can be sold immediately without requiring the full product ecosystem to exist.

This page must support that strategy by:
- making the offer tangible
- keeping scope fixed
- emphasizing speed and clarity
- focusing on business outcomes
- avoiding vague “agency” language

The page should feel more like a **diagnostic growth product** than a generic service page.

---

# 4. User Problem

The target user is often experiencing one or more of the following:

- They have a website, but it is not producing enough qualified leads
- They do not know if their site is helping or hurting conversions
- They suspect local visibility is weak
- They feel like leads are slipping through the cracks
- Their follow-up is inconsistent
- Their business still depends too much on referrals
- They are unsure what to fix first
- They want outside expert insight before investing in a larger project

They are not looking for abstract branding advice. They want clarity around:
- what is broken
- what it is costing them
- what to improve first

# 5. Target Audience

## Primary Audience
HVAC company owners, operators, or marketing decision-makers at small to midsize local HVAC businesses.

## Best-Fit Audience Traits
- Has an existing website

# 11. Functional Requirements

## Core Functional Requirements
1. The page must clearly communicate the audit offer above the fold.
2. The page must include at least one primary CTA in the hero section.
3. The page must explain what the audit includes in clear, scannable sections.
4. The page must display pricing clearly.
5. The page must explain deliverables and turnaround time.
6. The page must explain who the offer is for and not for.
7. The page must include FAQs to reduce friction and objections.
8. The page must include at least one final CTA near the bottom of the page.
9. The page must route users into purchase or intake flow.
10. The page must support analytics and conversion tracking.

## CTA Requirements
- Primary CTA text should be clear and action-oriented
- CTA examples:
  - Get My Audit
  - Claim Founder Pricing
  - Start My Audit
- CTA buttons should appear:
  - hero
  - mid-page after explanation
  - pricing section
  - final CTA section

## Checkout / Conversion Flow
The page should connect to either:
- a checkout flow, or
- a purchase form + payment step

Preferred user path:
1. User lands on page
2. User understands problem
3. User sees what the audit includes
4. User sees price and deliverables
5. User clicks CTA

# 21. Edge Cases / Risks

## Risks
- Page may feel too generic if HVAC specificity is not strong enough
- Offer may feel vague if “what’s included” is too abstract
- Too much copy could reduce scanning
- Too little trust could hurt conversion
- Founder pricing may attract low-intent users if not positioned well
- Scope confusion may happen if the page implies implementation is included

## Mitigations
- Keep scope explicit
- Use “not included” language where needed
- Keep section headings sharp and specific
- Repeat deliverable clarity
- Position the audit as diagnostic, not execution
- Use pricing and turnaround as confidence signals
